6. buatlah kalimat pronoun, mulai dari subject, objective, possesive ajentive, dan possesive pronoun.​


Tipe Pronouns

Ada lima (5) macam bentuk Pronouns dalam bahasa Inggris, yaitu subject pronouns, object/complement pronouns, possessives pronouns, possessives adjectives, dan reflexive pronouns. Berikut merupakan masing-masing penjelasannya ya, scroll down.

Subject Pronouns

Subject pronouns menempati posisi subjek dalam sebuah kalimat atau terletak setelah verb ‘be’. Di bawah ini merupakan daftar subjek pronouns, pelajari baik-baik.

I we

you you

he

she they

it

Contoh:

I am going to the store.

subject

We have lived here for twenty years.

subject

She and I have seen this movie before.

subject

George and I would like to leave now.

subject

We students are going to have a vacation.

subject

Note:

Subject pronoun juga dapat digunakan setelah than, as, dan that.

We, you, dan us dapat langsung diikuti oleh sebuah kata benda. Pada contoh di atas, we studends memberikan keterangan

Complement Pronouns

Object/complement pronouns menempati posisi objek atau pelengkap pada sebuah kalimat, baik di melengkapi sebuah verb atau sebuah preposisi.

me us

you you

him

her them

it

Contoh:

They called us on the phone.

object

The teacher gave him a bad grade.

object

John told her a story.

object

The policeman was looking for him.

object

To us, it seems like a good bargain.

After preposition

Mary is going to class with me.

After preposition

Meskipun demikian, ingatlah bahwa banyak preposisi yang dapat berfungsi sebagai kelas kata yang lain, seperti adverb atau conjunction.

Tetapi kamu juga harus memperhatikan kelas kata yang terdapat pada konteks, tidak hanya berdasar kepada pemahamanmu.

Contoh:

Janet will make her presentation after him.

Janet will make her presentation after he finished his speech.

(sebuah klausa yang terdiri dari subjek dan predikat. Pada klausa diatas he merupakan subjek dan finishes adalah kata kerja.

Possessives Adjectives

Possessive adjective berbeda dengan possessive pronouns. Possessive adjective berfungsi untuk memodifikasi daripada menggantikan noun. Possessive mengindikasikan kepemilikan.

my our

you your

his

her their

its

Contoh:

Emily is eating her dinner.

This is not my book.

The cat has injured its foot.

The boy broke his arm yesterday.

She forgot her homework this morning.

My food is cold.

Note: Its berbeda dengan It’s.

12. apa itu possesive adjective, possesive pronoun, dan apa itu reflexive pronoun


-Possessive adjective adalah determiner untuk menyatakan kepemilikan terhadap noun.

- Possessive Pronoun adalah kata ganti yang berfungsi untuk menunjukkan kepemilikan. Di dalam suatu kalimat, kata ini dapat menempati posisi sebagai subject, subject complement, atau direct object.

1) SUBJECT PRONOUNCE : subject / kata ganti pelaku yang selalu terletak pada awal kalimat

RUMUS : S + to be + Noun (ATAU) S + to be + Adjective

=> She is a dancer.

==> "She" sebagai subject

2) OBJECT PRONOUNCE : objek (kata ganti benda) dalam sebuah kalimat, sehingga diletakkan di akhir kalimat.

=> Mom bought me a cellphone.

==> "me" sebagai object

3) POSSESIVE ADJECTIVE : berfungsi untuk mengindikasi kepemilikan (kata ganti benda) yang selalu diikuti benda.

RUMUS : S + to be + Possesive Adjective + N

=> This is my bag.

4) POSSESSIVE PRONOUNS : kata ganti yang digunakan untuk mewakili atau menggantikan kepemilikan dari orang, benda atau binatang, yang tidak diikuti benda.

=> This red bag is your bag, the blue one is mine.

==> "mine" berfungsi sebagai kata ganti untuk "my bag"

36. Subjective pronoun Objevtive pronoun Possesive pronounPossesive pornoun Example and explanation


A subjective pronoun acts as the subject of a sentence—it performs the action of the verb. The subjective pronouns are he, I, it, she, they, we, and you.

He spends ages looking out the window.

After lunch, she and I went to the planetarium.

Objective Pronouns

An objective pronoun acts as the object of a sentence—it receives the action of the verb. The objective pronouns are her, him, it, me, them, us, and you.

Cousin Eldred gave me a trombone.

Take a picture of him, not us!

Possessive Pronouns

A possessive pronoun tells you who owns something. The possessive pronouns are hers, his, its, mine, ours, theirs, and yours.

The red basket is mine.

Yours is on the coffee table.

Demonstrative Pronouns

A demonstrative pronoun points out a noun. The demonstrative pronouns are that, these, this, and those.

That is a good idea.

These are hilarious cartoons.

A demonstrative pronoun may look like a demonstrative adjective, but it is used differently in a sentence: it acts as a pronoun, taking the place of a noun.

16. Principle of Philip B, Crosby


Jawaban:

Philip B. Crosby was a quality management expert known for introducing significant concepts in quality management. Crosby's principles, known as the "14 Steps to Quality Improvement" or "14 Points of Quality Management," were a framework he proposed for enhancing quality within an organization. Here are his principles:

1. Quality Management is Management: Understanding that quality improvement must be seen as an integral part of overall management.

2. Zero Defects: Striving to achieve zero defects as a standard in processes, products, or services.

3. Cost of Quality: Calculating the cost of quality deficiencies and considering prevention costs rather than just costs for fixing defective products.

4. Management Commitment: High-level commitment from management for quality improvement and organizational cultural change.

5. Education and Training: Providing necessary training to everyone in the organization to understand quality principles.

6. Zero Defects: Focusing on the goal of zero defects in every step or production process.

7. Continuous Improvement: Introducing continuous changes to improve quality.

8. Measurement System: Using measurement systems to assess success and quality performance.

9. Process Improvement: Continuously seeking ways to enhance production processes.

10. Quality Awareness: Developing an awareness of the importance of quality in every action taken.

11. Quality Teams: Forming quality teams responsible for managing improvement efforts.

12. Communication: Providing effective communication channels to understand quality issues and solutions.

13. Quality Projects: Undertaking specific projects aimed at enhancing quality.

14. Goals: Having long-term goals for continuously improving quality overall.

Crosby's approach emphasized prevention over correction, with a focus on quality from the start to the end of the process. These principles have served as a foundation for many organizations in implementing effective quality management practices.

35. pigeon hole principle itu gimana?



PIGEON HOLE PRINCIPLE (PRINSIP LUBANG MERPATI)
Pigen Hole Principle (Prinsip Lubang Merpati) mengatakan bahwa jika lebih dari n benda dimasukkan ke dalam n kotak, maka sedikitnya ada satu kotak yang berisi lebih dari satu benda. Secara umum bahwa jika ada lebih dari pn benda dimasukkan ke dalam n kotak maka sedikitnya ada satu kotak berisi lebih dari p benda.
Bentuk Lain : Jika n bilangan bulat m1, m2, m3, ⋅⋅⋅, mn memiliki rata-rata
[tex]\displaystyle\frac{m_{1}+m_{2}+m_{3}....+m_{n}}{n}\ \textgreater \ r-1[/tex]
maka sedikitnya satu di antara bilangan-bilangan bulat tersebut lebih besar atau sama dengan r

Contoh soal :
Jika ada 101 surat yang akan dimasukkan ke dalam 50 kotak pos, buktikan bahwa ada sedikitnya satu kotak pos berisi sekurang-kurangnya 3 surat.

jawab :
Jika seluruh kotak pos maksimal hanya berisi 2 surat, maka jumlah maksimal surat yang dapat masuk kotak pos adalah 100. Tetapi jumlah surat yang ada yaitu 101. Maka dapat dipastikan ada sedikitnya satu kotak pos berisi sekurang-kurangnya 3 surat.
